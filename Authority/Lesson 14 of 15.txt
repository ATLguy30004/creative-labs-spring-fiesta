Your site is live, your forms work, but Google doesn't know you exist yet. No sitemap, no meta descriptions, no schema markup. Search engines are guessing what your site is about, and they're probably guessing wrong.

This lesson installs all the SEO fundamentals using free Astro integrations. No paid plugins, no monthly fees. Just solid defaults that you manage by talking to Claude.

Before You Start
Your site needs to be live, even on a dev URL. Sitemaps and robots.txt depend on having an actual URL to reference. If you haven't deployed yet, do Lesson 10 first.

Also, create a new branch for this work. Call it something like seo.

Step 1: Set Your Site URL

Step 2: Sitemap

The Deploy Dance
Commit your SEO branch, publish it, and create a PR. Here's the honest truth: Cloudflare will probably fail the first build. That's normal.

Copy the error logs from Cloudflare, paste them into Claude, let it fix the issue, commit again, and wait for Cloudflare to retry. You might do this two or three times.

Real talk: I had to go back and forth a bit with deployment errors on this one. That's just what happens when you add multiple integrations at once. The point is that Cloudflare gives you detailed error logs, and those logs give Claude amazing context to work with. Each fix takes about 30 seconds.

Pro tip: If Claude gets stuck on deployment errors, remind it: "I'm on Cloudflare Workers, not Pages." This distinction matters for the build configuration and it's an easy thing for Claude to mix up.

Once the preview looks good, merge the PR, delete the branch, and sync your local repo.

Verify the basics: check your live site's title tag, visit /sitemap-index.xml, and confirm your pages are listed.
