SECTION 1: THE LANDSCAPE MATRIX
The Practical Paths (Execution Focus)
The "Bundle-First" UI: The top of the page doesn't sell "tacos"; it sells the "Elote King All-Access Pass" (Food + Bull Ride + Fast Pass).

Visual Menu Blocks: High-resolution, "food-porn" tiles with one-click "Add to Cart."

SMS Capture Loop: A "Text BULL to [Number]" popup for a 10% discount, building a broadcast list for the day of the event.

The "Fast-Pass" Upsell: A toggle at checkout: "Add $10 to skip the Bull Ride line."

Location Pin Integration: A live Google Maps embed with "Get Directions" prominent for the Atlanta crowd.

The Traps & Warnings
The "Decision Fatigue" Trap: If you offer 20 different menu items, pre-orders will tank. Stick to 5 high-margin "King’s Specials."

The Payment Friction: Use Apple Pay/Google Pay. If a user has to type their credit card number on a mobile phone while looking at a bull, they will quit.

The Asymmetric Plays
The "Bounty" Leaderboard: A live section showing "Confirmed Riders" and their "Goal Time." It gamifies the event before it starts.

The "Influencer Ticket": Create a "Refer a Friend" link on the Thank You page. If 3 friends buy, the original buyer gets their Elote free.

The "WOW" Factor
The "Bull-Cam" Tease: A grainy, high-energy 10-second clip of the bull bucking at night with a "You're Next" overlay.
